Where we play

S&B Streatham - S&B in central London - S&B online

You don’t have to live locally to be part of our club – several of our members live north of the river or outside London and only want to play team chess in central London where they work. But if you are within reach of Streatham, then we have more to offer.

S&B Streatham

Our club venue is the Café at St Thomas Church, Telford Avenue, SW2 4XW [insert Google Maps link]. We meet here most Tuesday evenings, 7.30 to 10.30, during the chess season from September to June. The Café is a 10-minute walk from Streatham Hill overground station, and slightly further from Balham underground station, as well as being on several bus routes along Streatham High Road. There is free on-street parking. We offer a full range of competitions, events and social evenings at the Café, as well as playing all our home matches in the Croydon Chess League (we have teams in all five divisions). During the 2025/26 season we are planning to expand the number of Tuesday sessions we hold at the Café.

S&B in central London

We play in two evening chess leagues based in central London: the London Chess League and the Central London Chess League. In the London Chess League we have teams in divisions 1, 2 and the Intermediate division. Matches usually start at 6.45 and most are played St Margaret’s Hall, 21 Old Ford Road, E2 9PL [insert Google Maps link] near Bethnal Green tube. In the Central London Chess League we have teams in divisions 1, 3 and 4. All matches start at 6.30 and are played at Churchill Gardens Tenants Association, Glasgow Terrace, SW1V 3AL [insert Google Maps link] near Pimlico tube.

S&B online

If you like to play from the comfort of your own screen we have two teams in the online 4NCL, divisions 1 and 3). From time-to-to time we run other online events, including club individual competitions.

Membership

How to join

How to join

If all you want is somewhere to play relaxed social chess, a warm welcome awaits you at the Chess at Streatham Library [insert link] group. This meets on Tuesday afternoons 4.30-7.30 and is an informal, drop-in group (you come and go as you please). There’s nothing to pay. Several S&B members help with organising these popular sessions. To play competitive rated chess you will need to join our club. As an S&B member you will be eligible to play for our league teams, subject to selection, and you will be also be able to participate in club competitions and other members-only events. See below for our current subscription rates. You will qualify for half price membership in your first season.

If you haven’t played competitive chess before, and you’re not sure whether it’s for you, you can play your first three games for free, and if you decide to continue playing we will ask you to join as a paying member. You will also need to join the English Chess Federation after you have played three games.

Club policies

Safeguarding - Privacy - Fair play online

Safeguarding

The safety and well-being of our members is paramount. S&B has a new management team in place which is currently reviewing all of our safeguarding procedures. Our aim is to adopt a comprehensive safeguarding policy, supported by experienced volunteers recruited from our membership to ensure effective implementation.

Privacy

The only information we obtain about you is that provided directly by you, when you join our club or ask to receive our emails and other communications, and it is used only for legitimate purposes. We do not collect personal data when you visit our websites. Click here to see our privacy policy [insert link to doc].

Fair play online

S&B does not condone cheating, which unfortunately is widespread online because of the ready availability of chess-playing software. We have teams in the 4NCL Online where all players are required to sign up to fair play guidelines, and detection arrangements and banning protocols are applied.
